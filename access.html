Estimation Methodology & Data Sources
1. Estimation Overview
This estimator projects annual revenue from two distinct payment mechanisms: (a) CMS ACCESS Model Outcome-based Alternative Payments (OAPs), a population-based per-member-per-month payment for qualifying chronic-condition beneficiaries under CMS Innovation Center authority; and (b) Medicare Physician Fee Schedule (PFS) billing codes for care management services (AWV, CCM, RPM, TCM, BHI) that are independently billable regardless of ACCESS participation.
All estimates are illustrative projections, not guarantees of payment. Actual revenue will depend on final CMS rule parameters, patient attribution, quality performance, documentation compliance, and practice-specific utilization patterns.
2. Input Methodology
Provider Identification: NPI is validated against the CMS NPPES registry (API v2.1). Provider name, credential, taxonomy, specialty classification, and practice location are sourced from the NPPES record. Entity type (Type 1 individual / Type 2 organizational) determines panel estimation logic.
Medicare Panel Size: When available, total unique beneficiaries are sourced from the CMS Medicare Physician & Other Practitioners Public Use File (PUF), CY2023 — the most recent available release (dataset UUID: 8889d81e-2ee7-448f-8713-f071038289b5). When CMS PUF data is unavailable (e.g., new providers, data suppression), panel size is estimated from specialty-specific benchmarks derived from MGMA DataDive 2024 and CMS Medicare Utilization data, capped at 5,000 for individual NPIs and min(specialty panel × 4, 6,000) for organizational NPIs.
Chronic Condition Prevalence: Default chronic prevalence rates are specialty-specific, derived from CMS Chronic Conditions Dashboard and peer-reviewed literature. The user may override via slider input (range: 20–95%). For ACCESS eligibility, 93% of chronic patients are assumed to meet ACCESS criteria, reflecting standard exclusions for hospice, ESRD, and institutional SNF stays per CMS-4205-F eligibility parameters.

These values are locked and marked as CMS-sourced. 5. Revenue Share Model
ACCESS OAPs: InPursuit retains a service fee as a percentage of ACCESS OAP revenue. The provider keeps 65% (Full Service tier), 70% (Supported tier), or 75% (Infrastructure Only tier). This fee covers application preparation, TETRA deployment, CMS compliance, quality reporting, and ongoing infrastructure costs.
Fee-for-Service Programs (AWV, CCM, RPM, TCM, BHI): The provider retains 100% of all FFS reimbursement. These are standard Medicare Part B codes billed under the provider's NPI. InPursuit's contribution is workflow optimization, patient identification, documentation support, and compliance infrastructure — not a revenue share arrangement. FFS optimization is included in the ACCESS service tier.

7. Key Assumptions & Limitations
Assumption 1 — ACCESS Eligibility: 93% of chronic patients are assumed to meet ACCESS criteria. Standard exclusions include hospice enrollment, end-stage renal disease (ESRD), institutional SNF stays >100 days, and Medicare Advantage enrollment. This rate is derived from CMS-4205-F eligibility parameters applied to the Medicare FFS Chronic Conditions Dashboard.

Assumption 2 — CMS Reimbursement Stability: All rates use CMS PFS Final Rule CY2025 values. Rates are subject to annual update via the CMS Physician Fee Schedule Final Rule and the Medicare conversion factor. Historical PFS changes have averaged ±2–3% annually over the past decade.

Assumption 3 — Patient Attribution: ACCESS OAP eligibility assumes standard CMS voluntary alignment or claims-based attribution. Actual attributed beneficiary counts may differ based on patient choice, competing ACO assignments, and CMS reconciliation processes.

Assumption 4 — Full-Year Enrollment: Revenue estimates assume 12 months of billing. For mid-year enrollment (e.g., Cohort 1 joining March 2026), first-year revenue should be prorated. This estimator shows annualized figures.

Assumption 5 — No Double-Counting: ACCESS OAP payments and FFS care management codes (CCM, RPM, etc.) are independently billable. CMS has confirmed that ACCESS OAPs do not replace or offset FFS billing for separately billable services. Revenue from both streams is additive.

Limitation 1 — CMS Data Lag: The most recent CMS PUF release is CY2023 (released 2025). Providers who have materially changed their billing patterns since CY2023 should use the slider overrides. CMS publishes PUF data on an approximately 18–24 month lag.

Limitation 2 — Data Suppression: CMS suppresses service-level data for HCPCS codes billed to fewer than 11 beneficiaries (per 45 CFR §164.514(b)). Suppressed programs default to national average estimates and are user-adjustable.

Limitation 3 — Quality Adjustments: ACCESS OAP payments may be subject to quality withhold and performance adjustment in later model years. This estimator does not apply a quality discount, which may reduce OAP payments by 0–10% depending on performance year and track.
